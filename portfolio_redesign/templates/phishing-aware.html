Stay safe

Five rules that actually work.

01
Check the full sender domain
Click the display name to reveal the real address. apple.com is real. appl3-verify.net is not. Read every character after the @.
02
Never click — type it yourself
If an email says verify your account, open a new tab and type the real URL directly. Never use an email button or link.
03
Urgency is a red flag
Real companies give you time. Any email demanding you act within hours is trying to stop you from thinking. The pressure is the attack.
04
Use a password manager
Password managers check the actual domain before autofilling. They won't fill your credentials on a fake site — this alone stops most phishing.
05
Call to verify
Unsure about an email from your bank or Apple? Call them using the number on their official website — never the number in the email.
