02
FastAPI backend analyzes
The Python backend checks domain age, SSL validity, seller verification status, redirect chains, and known scam databases.

Case Study

Why I built this

The problem

TikTok Shop made buying impulsive and frictionless — but it also removed every signal we used to judge a seller's trustworthiness. No eBay seller ratings. No Amazon reviews. Just a video, a price, and a link. Gen Z is clicking first and questioning later, and scammers know it. I wanted to put a security layer between the "add to cart" dopamine hit and the actual checkout.

Mia, 21
College student · TikTok Shop power user
"I've been burned twice. Once was a fake Stanley dupe that never arrived. Now I screenshot every seller page before I buy, but I don't actually know what I'm looking for."
Design decisions

The biggest challenge was communicating risk without causing panic. Security tools default to red everything — which teaches users to ignore warnings. FitCheck uses a traffic-light metaphor but softens it with Jost body copy and plain-English explanations. Every flag tells you what it means and why it matters, not just that something is wrong.

What I learned

Security UX is fundamentally a communication problem. The technical signals are well-understood — the hard part is translating them into language that creates the right level of concern without overwhelming a user who just wants a $12 phone case. That tension between friction and safety is what I want to keep designing around.
