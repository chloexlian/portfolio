About Me

Chloe Lian McIntosh

Cybersecurity Student · Web Developer · Security Educator

Background

I'm a third-year Computer Science student at Oregon State University, concentrating in cybersecurity and expected to graduate in June 2027. My academic path has been shaped by a genuine fascination with how systems break — and how to build them so they don't.

Beyond the technical side, I'm drawn to a question most security professionals don't ask: how do you get non-technical people to actually care about security? That interest sits at the intersection of cybersecurity, web development, and communications — and it's what makes me a different kind of candidate.

What I'm Working On

My flagship project is this portfolio itself — a Flask application secured with AES-256 encryption and session-based user authentication. I use it as both a practical implementation of secure coding principles and a platform for publishing cybersecurity education content aimed at general audiences.

I'm also pursuing the ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) and Google Cybersecurity Certificate to complement my academic training with industry-recognized credentials, and I actively participate in CTF (Capture the Flag) competitions to sharpen real-world offensive and defensive skills.

Professional Experience

While my internship experience is still ahead of me, my work history has sharpened skills that translate directly to security roles: data analysis, procedural compliance, accuracy under pressure, and the ability to identify and resolve discrepancies — all applied in high-volume, real-money environments.
